## Important Notes & Audit Risk Assessment

### High Certainty Deductions (Low Audit Risk)
- **Mortgage interest deduction** (with Form 1098)
- **401(k) contributions** (employer-verified)
- **Charitable donations** (to documented 501(c)(3) organizations)
- **HSA contributions** (if HDHP eligible)

### Medium Certainty Deductions (Medium Audit Risk)
- **Home office deduction** (requires proper documentation)
- **Business subscriptions** (must show business nexus)
- **Business meal deductions** (50% disallowance + documentation requirements)

### High Risk Deductions (High Audit Risk)
- **Commute mileage claimed as business** (most commonly audited)
- **Personal expenses mischaracterized as business** (aggressive position)
- **Entertainment/dining without documented business purpose**

### Critical Compliance Requirements
1. **Mileage Log Requirement:** IRS requires contemporaneous, detailed records. Estimates or retroactive logs do not satisfy audit requirements and create significant risk.
2. **Documentary Evidence:** All business expense deductions require supporting documentation (receipts, invoices, business records).
3. **Business Nexus:** Deduction must have clear connection to legitimate business activity, not personal use.
4. **Form Requirements:** Home office uses Form 8829; business income uses Schedule C.
